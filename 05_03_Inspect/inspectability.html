THE REALITY

The Lifetime Inspection Burden

That extra 30 minutes per inspection doesn't sound like much. Until you do the maths.

The Multiplication Effect

Consider a typical nuclear plant with 500 welds requiring periodic UT inspection. Each weld is inspected approximately 6 times over its 60-year service life.

500 welds × 6 inspections = 3,000 individual inspections

If your design adds 30 minutes to each inspection...

3,000 × 0.5 hours = 1,500 hours additional work

⚠️ Hidden Costs

Additional inspection time means: longer outages, higher radiation dose to personnel, increased labour costs at outage rates, and risk of extended outage if problems are discovered.

Regulatory Reality

LC28 requires inspections "within the intervals specified in the plant maintenance schedule." If your design makes inspections take longer than allowed, you have a compliance problem.

REGULATORY FRAMEWORK

Why Inspection Is Non-Negotiable

Required by site licence, expected by ONR, mandated by IAEA, specified in ASME codes.

UK REGULATION

Licence Condition 28

"The licensee shall make adequate arrangements for regular and systematic examination of all plant affecting safety."
  • Plant maintenance schedule required
  • Examinations by SQEP personnel
  • Written schemes specified

ONR EXPECTATIONS

Safety Assessment Principles

EMT.1: ISI requirements must be identified in the safety case.
EMT.5: Adequate access for EMIT activities.

INTERNATIONAL

IAEA SSR-2/1 Req. 29

"Design shall include provisions for access to allow safe execution of ISI."

ASME Section XI: 10-year inspection cycles since 1970.

The Design-Inspection Gap

ONR: "Components designed according to ASME III often cannot meet ASME XI NDE requirements." This gap persists for 60 years.

NDE METHODS

The Three Examination Types

Different methods have different access requirements. Tap each card to learn more.

👁️

VISUAL (VT)

Can inspectors see it?

▼
VT-1: Detailed Visual

Within 600mm, ≥30° viewing angle, 50 foot-candles lighting.

  • Clear line of sight
  • Adequate lighting
  • Access path for inspector

"Is there a clear sightline to every surface that matters?"

〰️

SURFACE (PT/MT/ET)

Can inspectors reach the surface?

▼
Liquid Penetrant / Magnetic Particle

Requires full surface access and cleanup capability.

  • Direct physical contact
  • Often requires weld crown grinding
  • Space for applicators

"Can an inspector physically reach every weld surface?"

🔊

VOLUMETRIC (UT/RT)

Can inspectors see inside?

▼
Ultrasonic Testing

Sound waves through material — the workhorse of nuclear inspection.

  • 75-100mm clear on BOTH sides
  • Surface finish Ra ≤ 6.3 μm
  • Multiple probe angles (45°, 60°, 70°)

"Is there space for probes on both sides, at multiple angles?"

Key Insight

Visual → Surface → Volumetric: access requirements become progressively more demanding. Design for the most stringent method.

SUMMARY

Key Takeaways

Design for the inspection reality — it's a regulatory requirement.

1

Design for the Method

Know what inspection your component requires. Design to accommodate it from the start.

2

Think in Decades

15 extra minutes × 6 cycles × 500 welds = massive cumulative impact over 60 years.

3

Can't Inspect = Can't Verify

Impossible inspection = ongoing safety question. Design for certainty.
